Overview

Eastern Medicine

The Successful Therapeutic Model

Eastern Medicine is the successful therapeutic model on a virtual parameter came into existence by the vision of “Shaheed Hakim Mohammed Said” (1920-1998). He set-up Hamdard Pakistan with his mission “Love Pakistan - Build Pakistan” in 1948. Rapidly, the herbal medicinal products of Hamdard became very familiar in treating the ailments. The phenomenal service for suffering humanity of Hamdard brand is a legendary part of the history in Pakistan. The name Hamdard however won a broad spectrum, when Hakim Sahib founded Hamdard University, in 1991 (now comprising seven different faculties) Madinat Al-Hikmah, in 1985, (the city of knowledge) and Bait Al-Hikmah (the central library & research house) in 1988. Thus, the establishment of a university which could enliven the intellectual tradition of educational institutions of the golden era past in Muslim civilization had always been his most cherished dream. We can say, it was the central point towards which all his endeavors were directed. Earlier in 1956 Mohtarma Fatima Jinnah inaugurated the Jamia Tibbia Sharqia Nazimabad. Hakim Sahib shifted that institution at the premises of university & merged with it. Later he transformed the same institution into the Hamdard College of Eastern Medicine, (now Faculty of Eastern Medicine) and then introduced the Bachelor of Eastern Medicine & Surgery (BEMS) degree program in 1994. He innovated state of the art research based integrated curriculum consisting of academic syllabus of both MBBS & Greco-Arab Medicine. An initiative which has filled the gap and produced such physicians, cognizant of both systems with advanced knowledge corelated with ancient philosophy. Comprising of Five years professional study period and One-year compulsory house job, the BEMS degree program is a brilliant combination of Greco-Arab Medicine with advanced knowledge of Modern Medicine, resulted into the success of a vision that inspired many universities in Pakistan to expand the umbrella of Eastern Medicine
